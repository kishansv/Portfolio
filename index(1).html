I built this satellite to capture what the human eye misses. By integrating an MQ-2 sensor array with RP2040 logic, I created a system that doesn't just 'see' the air—it decodes it. Whether it’s tracking a thermal spike or a whisper of smoke, the real thrill was engineering the SPI data link to ensure every millisecond of telemetry was locked onto the SD card. It’s not just a weather station; it’s a localized nerve center for environmental intelligence.
This is where I pushed the RP2040 to its limit to achieve a sub-millisecond response loop. While standard security systems wait for a software cycle to finish, I utilized hardware-level interrupts to trigger a physical alert in just 0.006s. It’s the purest realization of my design philosophy: minimizing the friction between a digital breach and a physical reaction. If the nerves aren't fast enough, the system isn't finished.
